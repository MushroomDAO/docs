## 1. Introduction

MushroomDAO is a Decentralized Autonomous Organization built by the co-creators of the Mycelium Protocol. Its mission is to explore and practice a new form of digital cooperation — one inspired by the 4.5-billion-year-old fungal network: decentralized, permissionless, and mutually beneficial.

**Mission:** Explore and practice the Mycelium Protocol. Build a digital future for ordinary people.

**Vision:** Provide components and community support (incubation, funding, tooling) to help innovators build and operate their own collaboration networks — strengthening the protocol's overall reach and resilience.

MushroomDAO operates on three pillars:
- **Protocol cooperator** — building and evolving the Mycelium Protocol together
- **Infrastructure provider** — open-source components for any community to use
- **Application incubator** — supporting projects that put the protocol to real-world use

## 2. Organizational Structure

MushroomDAO is not a company. It is a protocol-governed collective with four key roles:

### 2.1 Protocol Initiator — HyperCapital
HyperCapital is the organization that launched MushroomDAO. It holds 20% of GToken (4-year linear vesting) and provides:
- Initial funding for the first two years of operations
- Strategic support for commercial partnerships and regulatory navigation
- Business development for the ecosystem's commercial layer

HyperCapital acts as a long-term aligned partner, not a controller. Its 20% voting weight is bounded by DAO governance — it cannot unilaterally change core protocol parameters.

### 2.2 Protocol Vault (Treasury)
The community treasury receives:
- 5% of protocol fees from all community token issuances
- Gas sponsorship protocol revenue (10% of SuperPaymaster fees)
- Ecosystem grants and institutional partnerships

The treasury is governed by GToken holders. All disbursements require on-chain proposals that pass community vote.

### 2.3 Infrastructure Builders — AAStar
AAStar Community is the primary open-source team building the core infrastructure layer:
- **AirAccount** — ERC-4337 smart wallets with Passkey auth and social recovery
- **SuperPaymaster** — Decentralized gas sponsorship
- **SDSS (Rain Computing)** — Distributed computing and storage
- **CometENS** — ENS-compatible name resolution

AAStar receives grants from the Protocol Vault for open-source work. It operates independently from MushroomDAO governance.

### 2.4 Protocol Incubator — OpenNest
OpenNest is MushroomDAO's process-driven incubation framework. Projects entering OpenNest receive:
- Funding from the Protocol Vault
- Technical support from the AAStar infrastructure team
- Network access to the Mycelium Protocol community

OpenNest incubation is milestone-driven — projects receive tranches as they hit verifiable goals. Current incubated projects include: Arcadia, Chiang Mai Connect, Zu.Coffee.

### 2.5 Community Governance
All GToken holders participate in governance. See [Tokenomics](tokenomics-en.md) for full governance mechanics including proposal thresholds, quorum, and timelock.

## 3. DAO Capabilities

### 3.1 Digital Infrastructure Support
MushroomDAO provides every community member with:
- **Wallet-as-a-service:** AirAccount deployment with no seed phrase — onboarding in under 60 seconds
- **Gas-free transactions:** SuperPaymaster covers gas costs for all protocol interactions
- **Decentralized storage and compute:** SDSS provides censorship-resistant data and computing
- All accessible via **COS72** — the unified DAO/community tooling framework

### 3.2 Innovation Incubation
Via OpenNest, MushroomDAO supports builders with:
- Milestone-based grant funding from the Protocol Vault
- Technical collaboration with AAStar infrastructure team
- Community distribution and user acquisition support
- Legal and compliance guidance (especially for Thailand / SEA markets)

### 3.3 Local Market Activation
MushroomDAO has a strategic focus on Chiang Mai, Thailand as its first live sandbox:
- **CMUBA** (Chiang Mai University Blockchain Association) — academic partner, 3% GToken allocation
- **Arcadia** — on-chain loyalty gamification deployed at local merchants
- **Chiang Mai Connect** — local event and merchant discovery protocol
- **Phase 2 roadmap** — CMU Sandbox Report + University Partnership Agreement (2026-2027)

## 4. Revenue Model

| Stream | Mechanism | Beneficiary |
|---|---|---|
| Gas sponsorship fee | 10% of all SuperPaymaster transactions | Protocol Vault |
| Protocol issuance fee | 5% of all community PNTs issuance | Protocol Vault |
| NFT trading fee | 5% of mobile NFT minting and sales | Protocol Vault |
| Privacy data marketplace | "Touch me" — consent-based PCD data payments | Members + Vault |
| OpenNest project success | Share of incubated project revenue | Protocol Vault |
| GToken distribution | Initial unlock + ecosystem growth | DAO treasury |

Revenue flows into the Protocol Vault, then distributed via governance vote to: infrastructure grants, community incentives, and ecosystem development.

---

## 5. Relationship to Mycelium Protocol

MushroomDAO **is not** Mycelium Protocol — it is the primary organization building, testing, and governing it.

The Mycelium Protocol is open. Anyone can build on it, fork it, or extend it — without asking MushroomDAO. MushroomDAO simply has the most skin in the game as the founding organization.

This is intentional: the protocol should outlive any single organization, the way fungal networks outlive individual trees.
